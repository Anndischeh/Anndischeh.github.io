About me

I received my Master's in Electrical Engineering from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Tehran, Tehran, Iran.

Previously, I worked as a Visiting Research Scholar at EPFL (École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne) in Lausanne, Switzerland, where I further explored the applications of machine learning in data analysis.

I have worked as a Research Assistant at the University of Tehran, where I focused on different methods of antenna miniaturization based on geometric shapes. I also designed simplified antenna fabrication methods using 2D printed PCB boards, which were then converted into 3D antennas through an assembly process.

As part of my MS. research at the Antenna Measurement Laboratory at the University of Tehran, I focused on antenna miniaturization techniques and simulated radio frequency parameters using HFSS and CST software tools.

Currently, my research focuses on the development of machine learning and statistical methods to infer the representation of informative features in high-dimensional data.
